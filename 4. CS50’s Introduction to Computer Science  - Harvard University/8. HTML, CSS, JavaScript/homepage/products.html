Products from Beekeeping

The primary products of beekeeping are honey, beeswax, and pollen. Additionally, beekeepers may also sell queen bees, packaged bees, and beekeeping equipment. Some beekeepers may also offer honeycomb, propolis, and royal jelly. Some beekeepers also use bees for pollination services for crops like almond and blueberries.

Beeswax can be used to make a variety of products, including:

  • Candles: Beeswax candles burn longer and brighter than those made from other waxes, and they release a pleasant honey scent.
  • Soaps: Beeswax is often used as an ingredient in soaps to add a moisturizing effect.
  • Lip balms: Beeswax is used to make lip balms, as it creates a protective barrier on the skin that helps to prevent moisture loss.
  • Salves and ointments: Beeswax is used as a thickening agent in salves and ointments to create a protective barrier on the skin.
  • Lotions and moisturizers: Beeswax is used as an emulsifying agent in lotions and moisturizers to bind oil and water together.
  • Wood polish: Beeswax can be used to polish wood furniture, it can bring the natural shine of wood.
  • Cosmetics: Beeswax is used as an ingredient in cosmetics to thicken and stabilize formulations.
  • Art supplies: Beeswax is used in encaustic painting, a technique of painting with pigmented wax.
  • Sealing jars, bottles and canning: Beeswax can be used to seal jars and bottles, keep food fresh and prolong their shelf life.
